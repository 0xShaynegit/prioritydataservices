Why specialist cleaners matter

General cleaning contractors use synthetic filters that capture particles down to around 2.5 microns. They recirculate what they cannot capture back into the air. In a data centre, this redistributes contamination through your airflow system and onto your equipment. The facility looks clean. The contamination has moved.

HEPA-filtered specialist vacuums capture 99.97% of particles at the 0.3 micron threshold, which is the standard required for electronic environments. Our staff use antistatic equipment and follow procedures designed for live critical environments. No soapy water. No general contractors.

Our main goal is to minimise risk and unplanned outages. The lack of a scheduled maintenance program leads to equipment failure and loss of critical data and services. We work with you to create a tailor-made maintenance program suited to your facility's contamination profile and operational requirements.

The decontamination process

Four steps. Every job. No shortcuts.

  1. A

    Assessment

    On-site contamination audit. We inspect the facility, identify contamination sources, and document the full scope before a single piece of equipment is touched.

  2. B

    Protocol

    Tailored cleaning plan. VESDA suppression systems are isolated, airflow is mapped, and every procedure is agreed with your team in writing before work begins.

  3. C

    Execution

    HEPA-grade decontamination using antistatic equipment only. Subfloor voids, raised floor tiles, equipment inlets, and overhead cable trays are all addressed systematically. All contamination is contained, not redistributed.

  4. D

    Sign-Off

    The job is not complete until your authorised representative inspects the finished work and signs the job sheet. You are satisfied, or we are not done.

What we clean

Every area of the facility that accumulates contamination, not just the surfaces you can see.

Raised Floor
Subfloor Void

The subfloor void is the primary contamination reservoir in most data centres. Dust, debris, and zinc whisker particles accumulate undisturbed for years and are then drawn up through floor tiles into equipment by cooling airflow. This is where we start.

Primary source
02

Equipment Inlets
and Exhausts

Server and rack equipment inlets are decontaminated without powering down the facility wherever possible. Where individual components require isolation, this is agreed in the protocol stage and scheduled to minimise operational impact.

03

CRAC Units and
Cooling Systems

Computer Room Air Conditioning units are cleaned internally including belts, coils, and filter housings. Contaminated CRAC units are a secondary contamination source that continuously reintroduces particles to the room.
