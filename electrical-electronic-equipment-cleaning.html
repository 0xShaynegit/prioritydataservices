Cleaning extends equipment life

Priority Data Services cleans all types of electrical and electronic equipment. Most equipment is never maintained, and this lack of maintenance leads to failure of components or the equipment itself. Regular professional cleaning removes the contamination that accelerates wear.

Sanitisation of equipment also reduces the level of bacteria associated with multi-user devices. Studies consistently find that frequently touched surfaces in workplaces carry significant bacterial loads. Regular sanitisation every three months is recommended for high-touch multi-user equipment in any environment where hygiene is a concern.

Professionally cleaned equipment presents better to clients and customers. It also creates a culture where people take responsibility for the condition of their surroundings, which has broader workplace benefits beyond the equipment itself.

The cleaning process

Contamination removed. Surfaces sanitised. Equipment documented.

  1. A

    Scope and Assessment

    We assess each item of equipment to determine access, cleaning method, and any handling requirements specific to that equipment type. Items with physical damage or conditions that preclude cleaning are flagged before work begins.

  2. B

    Contamination Removal

    External contamination is removed using HEPA-filtered vacuuming and microfibre wipes. Internal contamination where accessible is addressed using appropriate tools. We do not use compressed air or solvent-based products that could damage equipment or redistribute contamination.

  3. C

    Sanitisation

    High-touch surfaces are sanitised using approved antistatic sanitiser appropriate to the equipment type. This is particularly important for multi-user equipment in shared workplaces and public-facing environments.

  4. D

    Documentation

    Completed work is documented. Photographic records are available on request. Any equipment conditions observed during cleaning that indicate underlying issues are reported to your representative.

Frequently asked questions

How often should electrical equipment be professionally cleaned?

For most shared-use office and IT equipment, every six to twelve months is appropriate. High-touch surfaces in environments with hygiene requirements, such as medical facilities, food service, or high-traffic public spaces, benefit from quarterly sanitisation. Equipment in environments with higher contamination loads requires more frequent attention.

Can you clean equipment on-site without removing it?

Yes. We work on-site for most equipment types. Items that require more thorough internal access may need to be powered down and isolated, but external cleaning and sanitisation is typically done in place without disrupting operations.

Do you clean medical or laboratory equipment?

Yes. We have experience cleaning in hospital departments, pathology laboratories, radiology suites, and other specialist medical environments. We work within the infection control protocols of those facilities and can provide documentation of the cleaning process for compliance purposes.

What products do you use on electronic equipment?

We use antistatic cleaning solutions appropriate to electronic equipment surfaces. We do not use general-purpose cleaning products, solvents, or anything that could damage equipment finishes or internal components. Products are selected based on the equipment type and manufacturer guidance where available.

Can you provide a regular scheduled service?

Yes. Scheduled maintenance agreements are available for organisations that want regular cleaning visits without the overhead of organising each visit separately. Contact us to discuss your equipment inventory and preferred schedule.
